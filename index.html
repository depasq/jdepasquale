About Me

I work as a Visualization Specialist in the office of Communication and Public Engagement for NASA's Chandra X-ray Observatory at the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory. If you've ever seen a space image with X-ray data in it, you've probably seen my work. For as long as I can remember, I've been fascinated by the immensity and complexity of the Universe and our search for meaning within it.
Having a background in both art and science has proven to be a unique skill set in which art and aesthetics inform the data preparation and image processing of astronomical data to create press quality imagery in support of Chandra's outreach activities.
I am currently enrolled in the Harvard Extension School's Digital Media Design Master's Program.
For more details, please refer to my portfolio below, or see my CV.

Visualization/Data Specialist


I have worked in data analysis and visualization for the past 15 years. In 2001, I accepted a position as a data analyst and calibration scientist for NASA's Chandra X-ray Observatory after graduating from Villanova University with a Bachelor of Science degree in Astronomy & Astrophysics. I spent 8 years in data analysis and observatory operations before switching to the office of Communication and Public Engagement to support the press office in the creation of press release imagery and supporting materials.

All Chandra press releases and associated imagery can be found at the main Chandra site: chandra.si.edu

Music & Audio Recording/Engineering

Coding


During my first 8 years with NASA as a calibration scientist for Chandra, I used PERL, IDL, Python, C++, FORTRAN and UNIX scripting in my daily work to streamline my workload and develop code for several large projects. Although my public outreach work has me focused on working in the Adobe Creative Suite, I've created opportunities to continue coding in Python and more recently JavaScript.

  • I was first tasked with creating a spacecraft limit monitoring system capable of interpreting real-time telemetry flow from the spacecraft and sending out email and page alerts if any spacecraft systems exceeded previously set thermal or electrical limits. This system is still in use today.

  • I developed custom software for analyzing data from the telescope to calibrate the spectral response of the spacecraft's main camera system. Additionally, I developed software designed to maximize the efficiency of the spacecraft observation time through more accurate modeling of the radiation and thermal environments of the telescope.

  • One of my first large projects in public outeach was to accurately tag over 600 images with astronomically-relevant metadata. This involved working with a team of scientists across NASA to develop a new metadata standard utilizing Adobe's exisiting extensible metadata platform XMP to incorporate astronomical information in press release imagery.
    As a result of this project, all press imagery from Chandra contains metadata with detailed information about how the image was constructed, including the object's position on the sky. This opens up a new universe of possibility with these images. For example, using the image metadata, I developed a new javascript tool called Chandra SkyMap, providing the public with an intuitive, user-driven exploration of the sky as seen through Chandra's X-ray eyes.
