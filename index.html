About Me

I work as a visualization specialist in the office of Communication and Public Engagement for NASA's Chandra X-ray Observatory at the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory. If you've ever seen a space image with X-ray data in it, you've probably seen my work. For as long as I can remember, I've been fascinated by the immensity and complexity of the universe and our search for meaning within it.

Having a background in both art and science has given me a unique skill set. I use art and aesthetics to prepare astronomical data for the creation of press-quality imagery in support of Chandra's outreach activities.

I am currently enrolled in the Harvard Extension School's Digital Media Design Master's Program. For more details, please refer to my portfolio below, or see my CV.

Visualization Specialist


I have worked in data analysis and visualization for the past 15 years. I spent 8 years in data analysis and observatory operations before switching to the office of Communication and Public Engagement to support the press office in the creation of press release imagery and supporting materials.


Highlights

  • Astrophotgraphy: my interview with Smithsonian Education; my reddit AMA
  • Public Speaking: given technical lectures at image processing workshops; given lectures to the general public and school groups about astronomy and the story of the Chandra X-ray Observatory
  • Collaborations: with Harvard Art Museums, developed an interactive exhibit with colleagues in the Straus Center for Conservation and Technical Studies exploring the connections between the use of light in both art conservation and astronomy; co-hosted an image processing workshop at Harvard with PixInsight (Summer 2014)
  • Online Content Creation: astrophotography tutorials called openFITS geared towards novice users; supporting podcast videos for Chandra press releases; blog posts such as Astronomy Night on the National Mall; interactive JavaScript tools
  • 3D Printing: expert in 3D printing for outreach purposes such as printing a model of a supernova explosion, or utilizing 3D printing and the Arduino electronics prototyping platform to shed light on the question of how an X-ray telescope such as Chandra focuses X-ray light

Music & Audio Recording/Engineering


Through my experiences with my band, Riding Shotgun over the last 14 years, I've learned a lot about audio engineering for live and studio performance. I built a mobile recording unit enabling the band to capture high fidelity recordings in a variety of locations.

As an instrumental trio, effects play a big role in creating a large sound for the band using live looping and layered effects. I designed an efficient guitar rig that can replicate studio sounds yet is easily and quickly set up and broken down.

Coding


As a calibration scientist for Chandra, I used PERL, IDL, Python, C++, FORTRAN and UNIX scripting in my daily work to streamline my workload and develop code. Although my public outreach work has me focused on working in the Adobe Creative Suite, I've created opportunities to continue coding in Python and more recently JavaScript.

  • Image Metadata Project: I accurately tagged the entire Chandra image archive with astronomically relevant metadata. This was part of a collaboration with scientists across NASA to develop new metadata standards utilizing Adobe's exisiting extensible metadata platform (XMP).
    All press imagery from Chandra contains metadata with detailed information about how the image was constructed, opening the door for new and innovative tools for exploration. For example, I developed Chandra SkyMap in JavaScript, providing the public with an intuitive, user-driven exploration of the sky as seen through Chandra's X-ray eyes.
